Six ways to open the site

Same product, six genuinely different opening moves: promise, letter, hearsay, friend, mystery, identity. Flip through with the arrows at the bottom of each page (or your keyboard's left/right keys).

Make this the trip that counted
1 · The trip that counted

The promise, tuned. Cinematic full-bleed painting, gold CTA. Subtitle leads with "a way to experience Dubrovnik like the people who chose to stay."
“Everything I wish I’d known”
2 · The letter

Intimate, editorial paper. The guide's one-line confession of his own mistakes as the hook. Framed painting with a postmark stamp.
You've heard it's beautiful
3 · The hearsay

The Malkovich move. Minimal deep navy, giant type, no image. Understated dare: "this is about everything else."
The friend you wish you had here
4 · The friend

Product-led and instantly understandable. Real chat bubbles floating on the page, WhatsApp-green CTA "Say hi."
There are two Dubrovniks
5 · The two cities

Mystery and wonder. The celestial night painting, the guidebook city vs. the locals' city, CTA "Open the hidden world."
Tourists take photos. Storyhunters take stories.
6 · The Storyhunter

Identity manifesto, pure typography. You're not buying a guide, you're becoming a kind of traveler. CTA "Become a Storyhunter."

These are hero screens only, wired to the real WhatsApp link. Whichever direction wins (or whichever mix: one's headline, another's subtitle, a third's design) gets built into the full homepage.
